Chapter 3

Origins of spring

The term spring is a relatively new name for the season. Previously it was referred to as 'lent'. Which a lot of people still use today when referring to the 40 days leading up to easter. It was around the 14th century when this time of year started to be called 'springing time' which refers to how plants 'spring' up from the ground at this time of year. Around the 14th century people shortened this down to 'spring-time' and eventually just 'spring' in the 16th century.
